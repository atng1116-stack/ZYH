Yuhang Zhang

Guangzhou Academy of Fine Arts (GAFA) — Bachelor of Fine Arts in Sculpture
Media: interactive installations, games, sound, light; materials: silicone, wood, liquids
My research focuses on humanity and society in the multimedia era. Starting from the contemporary internet and media landscape, I seek to observe and contemplate the subtle shifts in human behavior and emotion—those moments and states amplified, distorted, or obscured by the digital tide. I believe these transformations not only reshape individual emotional structures but also subtly propel societal change. In my creative practice, I typically begin with real-world issues, seeking answers through speculative and experimental expression. I employ multimedia technology as my primary medium—including interactive installations, games, sound, and light—while integrating traditional physical materials like silicone, wood, and liquids. My goal is to construct experiential spaces that are both interactive and imbued with a digital sensibility. By conveying poetic virtual imagery and visual stimuli, I aim to provoke sensory responses in viewers—even a hint of “pain.” This “pain” is not negative but serves as a wake-up call, prompting people to reexamine social events and human conditions within the digital age. Ultimately, it fosters reflection and insight into both the self and society.
